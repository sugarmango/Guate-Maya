Antigua Guatemala


Antigua Guatemala or Old Guatemala is also known as "Antigua". Antigua was the third city to home the capital city of Guatemala founded in 1543, which lasted for 200 years until the capital city was moved once again to present-day Guatemala City. Antigua has a Spanish-Baroque architecture thanks to the Spanish Conquitsador's who established the kingdom of Guatemala there, which included the current states of Guatemala, Belize, El Salvador, Honduras, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, and Chiapas, Mexico. Antigua was established as head of the Real Audiencia of Guatemala in 1549. Antigua sufferred the catasthrophic consequences of many earthqueakes over the centuries so there are several ruins of buildings from the Colonial era. However, Antigua is the best preserved Colonial city in Central America and it attracts several local and foreign tourist froms around the world due to it's charming beauty.
