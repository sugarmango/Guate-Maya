Guate Maya


Guatemala originally named Cuauhtēmallān in Nahuatl, which means "place of many trees" is also known in the modern day Guatemala as "the country of the eternal Spring" due to its tropical weather and beautiful green landscapes. Guatemala is also home to 37 volcanoes with 4 of them currently active. Guatemala is culturally diverse and the home to various ethnic groups including Mayan, Xinca, Garifuna, Afro-Guatemalan, Ladino, and foreigners from all around the world. Even though Spanish is the official language in the country, there are 22 Mayan languages and dialects, as well as Garifuna and Xinca.

Guatemala is located at the heart of the Mayan civilization and a true treasure hidden in plain sight. At Guate Maya we are passionate about sharing the wonders of our country with the world for a reasonable fee. We currently offer the top 3 tourist locations in Guatemala with the Tikal ruins in Peten, Quetzaltenango, and Semuc Champey in Alta Verapaz coming up in 2022.
